## When to use

- You want your AI to write a document — proposal, report, meeting note — and **save it as a page in your Notion**.
- You run your knowledge base in Notion and want the initial drafting of each page off your plate.
- You want to use existing Notion pages as context for the new one.

## What it does

Bundles two pieces that reinforce each other, in one install:

- **Notion (the connector)** reads and creates pages in your workspace — so the AI can read your prior notes to stay consistent.
- **Doc Co-Authoring (the skill)** writes proposals, reports, and share-ready documents, with coherence, evidence, and tone passes, and its limits (it doesn't replace a lawyer on legally binding contracts).

**A real example:** you walk out of a team meeting with loose points. You say *"draft a meeting note from these points, following the format of my Projects base, and save it there"*. Notion gives it a prior note as a template, Doc Co-Authoring writes the new one with that structure and tasks split by owner, and saves it as a page in the right base. Your team opens Notion and the note is already there.

## How to use

1. Install the Plugin and connect your Notion workspace.
2. Ask: *"write a progress report from these notes and save it to Notion"*.
3. Review the document — it lands as a page where you point it.

## Why the bundle works

The writing skill alone gives you text you then copy-paste into Notion by hand. The connector alone creates pages, but doesn't write them with judgment. Together: the AI reads your prior pages to keep the style, writes the new one, and saves it where it belongs — from idea to finished page, no jumps.

## Limits

- It writes review-ready drafts, not final content without your eye.
- It doesn't replace legal, accounting, or medical advice; for regulated topics, get professional review.
- Requires connecting your Notion workspace; it only sees and creates what that access allows.
