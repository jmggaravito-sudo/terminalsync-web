## When to use

- It's end of month and you owe investors the update from 5 days ago.
- Your data is scattered (Stripe, dashboards, notes) and assembling it takes 3 hours you don't have.
- You want a consistent template month-over-month so they can compare.

## What it does

From the raw numbers you paste:

- **TL;DR** (3 bullets, 10-second read)
- **Highlights** (what moved the needle this month — metric + why)
- **Lowlights** (what didn't work — no hiding, no victim mode)
- **KPIs** (table with MRR/users/runway vs last month)
- **Asks** (intros, hires, advice — specific, actionable)

Blocks: never uses "we're crushing it", "exciting times ahead", "the future is bright". Zero hype.

## How to use

1. Paste the numbers (Stripe export, Mixpanel, whatever you have).
2. Tell it what happened qualitatively this month in 2-3 sentences.
3. Returns the full email in the format you gave it the first month (remember to pass the original template).

## Best for

Early-stage founders reporting to 10-30 angels or a seed fund, founders who want month-over-month consistency, teams sharing the update with their board too.
