## When to use

- Sales from repeat customers are slipping and you suspect people who used to buy just stopped.
- You want a **list of who to reach out to** plus the actual message, not just "do a win-back campaign."
- You're deciding where to spend a small retention budget and don't want to waste it on customers who are already gone.
- You want the reusable capability a scheduled win-back run can call — this skill finds and drafts; sending stays with you or the connected email/CRM.

Works from a connected store/CRM (Shopify, Square, HubSpot) or a pasted order/customer export. If you only have a list of emails with no purchase dates, it will say it can't tell who is dormant and ask for order dates.

## What it does

- **Defines "dormant" for your business**: sets an inactivity window from your actual buying rhythm (a weekly-consumable buyer is dormant at 60 days; an annual purchase isn't), and shows the window it chose.
- **Finds and ranks the dormant**: who lapsed, how long ago, what they used to spend, and how often — so a past champion outranks a one-time discount shopper.
- **Splits by worth-reviving**: high-value recently-lapsed (act now), steady mid-value (nudge), low-value one-timers (cheap automated touch only), likely-gone (don't overspend).
- **Drafts the reactivation message per group**: a short, non-desperate message with a reason to return (what's new, a fair offer only where the margin allows), matched to the group — not one generic "we miss you."
- **The verdict (always close with this)**: a **0–100 score** for how strong this win-back opportunity is (size and value of the revivable group vs. data quality); a traffic light with a threshold (🟢 80+ a real revivable base, launch it; 🟡 50–79 worth doing on the top group only; 🔴 <50 too few/too old or missing dates — don't spend yet); **the single group to contact first**; and the reminder that the score reflects past behavior, not a promise they'll come back.

It won't invent a discount that kills your margin, won't promise a return rate, and won't treat "has an email" as "is revivable."

## How to use

1. Connect your store/CRM or paste an export with customer id/email, order dates, and totals.
2. Tell it your normal buying rhythm or product type so it sets the dormancy window sensibly.
3. Ask for the dormant list ranked by worth-reviving, with counts per group.
4. Ask it to draft the message for the top group; give it any real offer limits (max discount, what's new) so it doesn't over-promise.
5. Send through your connected email/CRM (or hand the list to a win-back loop), then measure who actually returned before scaling.

## Best for

Store and service owners with repeat customers and no dedicated retention team. Best when you have dated purchase history per customer; weaker for one-purchase businesses, brand-new stores, or lists without purchase dates.
