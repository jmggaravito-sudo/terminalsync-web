## When to use

- You're tempted to run a store-wide sale but suspect you'd be handing a discount to customers who'd have paid full price.
- You want a promo **aimed at one segment** — lapsed buyers, a slow product, first-time-to-second purchase — with the discount sized to the goal.
- You want the offer, the target list, and the guardrails, not just "run 20% off."
- You want the reusable capability a promo loop can call: it designs the offer and picks the segment; issuing codes and sending run through your connected store.

Works from a connected store (Shopify) or a segment you already have (e.g. from RFM Segmentation) plus your margin. Without margin and a defined segment, it will ask for them before proposing a discount.

## What it does

- **Ties the promo to a goal and a segment**: clear a slow product, revive lapsed buyers, push a first-time buyer to a second order, move overstock — and targets only the segment that serves that goal, so you don't discount the whole base.
- **Sizes the discount to protect margin**: proposes the smallest offer likely to work (percentage, amount, bundle, free shipping, gift-with-purchase) and shows the margin impact and break-even units, flagging when a discount would sell at a loss.
- **Avoids subsidizing sure buyers**: steers away from blanket discounts your regulars would trigger anyway, and prefers targeted codes, minimums, or segment-gated offers.
- **Adds guardrails and drafts the offer**: expiry, per-customer limits, exclusions, and honest copy (a real reason for the offer, no fake urgency), plus the segment to send it to.
- **The verdict (always close with this)**: a **0–100 score** for whether this promo is worth running (margin room, segment fit, whether the goal justifies the giveaway); a traffic light with a threshold (🟢 80+ well-targeted and margin-safe, run it; 🟡 50–79 run it lean on the tightest segment; 🔴 <50 it mostly subsidizes buyers you'd keep, or kills margin — don't run it); **the single highest-return offer/segment pairing**; and the reminder that the score reflects your stated margin and goal, not guaranteed incremental sales.

It won't propose a below-cost discount without flagging it, won't blanket-discount when a targeted offer is better, and won't invent urgency.

## How to use

1. Connect Shopify or bring a segment (e.g. from RFM Segmentation) plus your margin and average order value.
2. State the goal: *"Clear 200 units of last season's stock"* or *"get first-time buyers to a second order."*
3. Ask for the offer sized to protect margin, the target segment, the guardrails, and the break-even.
4. Check the margin math before issuing codes; if it sells at a loss, ask for a smaller or non-discount offer.
5. Run it on the target segment only, measure incremental sales (not total), and keep the guardrails on.

## Best for

Store owners who reach for discounts and want them targeted and margin-safe instead of store-wide. Best when you know your margin and can define a segment (RFM helps); weaker when you can't segment or don't know your unit economics.
