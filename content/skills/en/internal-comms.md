## When to use

- You need to communicate a change, incident, launch, policy update, strategy shift, restructuring, or difficult company news.
- You want a message that is clear, human, and specific to the audience instead of vague corporate language.
- You need the assistant to identify what employees will ask next: timing, ownership, impact, action required, and where to get help.
- You want to review tone before sending: too cold, too defensive, too vague, too optimistic, or too long.

For layoffs, restructurings, performance actions, compensation, benefits, discrimination, workplace investigations, or regulated HR topics, it can help frame a draft and checklist. It does not replace legal, HR, compliance, or employee-relations review.

## What it does

Builds internal communication through a process:

- **Context intake**: situation, audience, decision status, what can be disclosed, what cannot, and desired outcome.
- **Audience mapping**: what each group needs to know, feel, do, and ask next.
- **Message structure**: TL;DR, why now, what changes, what does not change, actions, owner, timeline, and support path.
- **Tone calibration**: direct, empathetic, accountable, calm, or celebratory depending on the situation.
- **Risk pass**: flags vague claims, false reassurance, blame, missing owners, confidential details, and sensitive HR/legal/compliance areas.

It should ask for clarification when facts, approvals, dates, or affected groups are unclear. It should not invent promises, legal positions, compensation details, or executive approvals.

## How to use

1. State the situation, audience, sensitivity level, send channel, and goal of the message.
2. Say what is confirmed versus still pending.
3. Ask for a draft plus risk review: *"Use Internal Comms. Draft this announcement and flag HR/legal/compliance concerns."*
4. Review the risk notes before sending, especially for people-sensitive topics.
5. For layoffs, restructurings, investigations, benefits, or policy changes, route the final draft through the appropriate human reviewers.

## Best for

Founders, managers, comms leads, People/HR partners, and operators who need structured internal messages. Strongest for status updates, incident post-mortems, launches, and change announcements; requires extra human review for sensitive employment or compliance matters.
