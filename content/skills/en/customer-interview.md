## When to use

- You ran 20 interviews and the doc is 80 pages no one will read.
- You want to decide what to build and need patterns, not individual quotes.
- You're presenting findings to the team and your deck can't be "look how many conversations we had".

## What it does

From the transcripts:

- **Patterns** (the 5-7 things appearing more than 3 times, ranked by frequency + emotional intensity)
- **Outliers that matter** (1-2 accounts that said something unique but worth hearing)
- **Selected quotes** (1 per pattern, the most representative, not the funniest)
- **Implicit decisions** ("if this is real, you should [concrete action]")

Blocks: doesn't deliver "ideas to validate" — delivers what's ALREADY validated by the dataset.

## How to use

1. Paste the transcripts (plain text, not audio — run your transcriber first).
2. Tell it the context: *"We're looking for product-market fit in X segment, target ARR is $Y"*.
3. Returns the prioritized report for your next planning.

## Best for

PMs and founders running discovery, UX teams before a design sprint, growth teams ranking experiments by qualitative signal.
