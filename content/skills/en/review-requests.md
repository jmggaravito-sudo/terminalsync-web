## When to use

- You get very few reviews even though customers seem happy, and you want more without nagging everyone.
- You want to ask **the right buyers at the right time** — after a product has actually arrived and had time to be used — not the moment they check out.
- You want the message and the send list, not just "remember to ask for reviews."
- You want the reusable capability a post-purchase loop can call: it picks who to ask and drafts the ask; you (or the connected email/store) send it.

Works from a connected store (Shopify, Square) or a pasted order export. If you have no delivery/fulfillment dates, it will say it can only guess timing and ask for order or delivery dates.

## What it does

- **Picks the good moment**: estimates when the product was received and adds a use-it-first buffer sized to the product (consumable vs. durable), so the ask lands after value, not before.
- **Filters to likely-happy buyers**: delivered and not refunded/returned, no open support issue it can see, ideally repeat buyers — and excludes anyone with a recent complaint so you don't invite a bad review.
- **Drafts an honest ask**: a short, specific request (mentions the actual product), easy to say no to, with a direct link placeholder to where the review goes — no pressure, no scripted five-star wording.
- **Keeps it compliant**: never offers money or discounts *in exchange for a positive review*, never writes fake reviews, and flags that incentivized reviews break most platforms' rules (a small thank-you for *any honest* review is called out as the only gray-area option, with the caveat).
- **The verdict (always close with this)**: a **0–100 score** for how ready this review push is (size of the eligible happy-buyer pool vs. data quality); a traffic light with a threshold (🟢 80+ solid pool, send it; 🟡 50–79 send to the cleanest sub-group first; 🔴 <50 too few eligible or missing dates — fix the data first); **the single best moment/group to start with**; and the reminder that the score reflects who it could see, not how many will actually review.

It will not fabricate reviews, will not ask unhappy or refunded customers, and will not promise a rating.

## How to use

1. Connect your store or paste an order export with order/delivery dates, refund/return status, and product.
2. Tell it the product type so it sets the "give it time to be used" buffer sensibly.
3. Ask for the eligible list plus the recommended send timing per group.
4. Ask it to draft the request and paste your real review link; keep it honest and easy to decline.
5. Send through your connected email/store (or hand the list to a review loop); watch for and route any complaints away from the ask.

## Best for

Store owners and service providers who deliver a good product but under-collect reviews. Best when you have order/delivery dates and refund status; weaker when you can't tell what was delivered or who was unhappy.
