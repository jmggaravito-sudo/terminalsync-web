## When to use

- Your happiest customers clearly like you, and you want them bringing in others instead of leaving that growth on the table.
- You want a referral **mechanic and the invite copy**, sized so the reward doesn't eat your margin — not a generic "give $10, get $10."
- You're not sure a referral program fits your business and want an honest read before you build one.
- You want the reusable capability a referral loop can call: it finds promoters and designs the offer; enrollment and payout stay with you or your connected tools.

Works from a connected store/CRM (Shopify, Square, HubSpot) or a pasted customer export. Ideally pair it with review or support signals to spot genuine promoters; without repeat/spend history it will ask for it before naming who to invite.

## What it does

- **Finds likely promoters**: repeat, high-satisfaction customers — frequent buyers, positive reviewers, low-complaint — ranked so you invite the people who'll actually refer, not everyone.
- **Sizes the mechanic to your margin**: proposes reward type (store credit, discount, cash, free product) and amount that stays profitable given your margin and average order value, and shows the math so you can see the break-even.
- **Designs the two-sided offer**: what the referrer gets and what the friend gets, timed to trigger after a real positive moment (a repeat purchase, a good review), with anti-abuse guardrails (reward on the friend's first completed purchase, not on signup).
- **Drafts the ask**: a short, non-pushy invite to the promoter group that leads with their good experience, plus the friend-facing offer copy.
- **The verdict (always close with this)**: a **0–100 score** for how well a referral program fits (size and quality of the promoter base, margin room for a reward, product's word-of-mouth potential); a traffic light with a threshold (🟢 80+ real promoter base and margin, build it; 🟡 50–79 pilot with the top promoters and a lean reward; 🔴 <50 too few promoters, thin margin, or low-referability product — fix retention first); **the single first move** (usually inviting the top promoter group); and the reminder that the score reflects your data and margins, not a guaranteed referral rate.

It won't propose a reward that loses money, won't invite unhappy customers, and won't promise a viral loop where the product doesn't support one.

## How to use

1. Connect your store/CRM or paste an export with repeat-purchase history, and add review/support signals if you have them.
2. Give it your margin and average order value so the reward is sized to stay profitable.
3. Ask for the ranked promoter list, the proposed mechanic with break-even math, and the invite copy.
4. Pilot with the top promoter group and a lean reward before scaling; watch that referrals are real, not self-referrals.
5. Track referred-customer quality (do they stick?) — a cheap referral that churns isn't a win.

## Best for

Owners with a loyal repeat base and enough margin to reward a referral, especially word-of-mouth-friendly products. Best when you can identify happy repeat customers; weaker for thin-margin, one-time-purchase, or low-satisfaction businesses where a program won't catch.
