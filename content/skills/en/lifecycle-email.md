## When to use

- You send one generic newsletter to everyone and know a new subscriber, a first-time buyer, and a fading customer each need a different message.
- You want **written, editable email copy per stage** you can drop into Gmail or your CRM — not a black-box automation.
- You're setting up (or fixing) a welcome / onboarding / re-engagement flow and want the words, the order, and the timing.
- You want the reusable drafting capability a lifecycle loop can call: it decides the stage and writes the email; the actual sending stays with you or your connected email/CRM.

Works from a connected list (HubSpot, Gmail contacts) with signup/purchase context, or a pasted list with stage hints. With no stage signals (no signup date, no purchase history), it will ask what stage each group is in rather than guess.

## What it does

- **Maps people to a stage**: new subscriber, onboarding, first-purchase follow-up, active/repeat, fading/at-risk — using signup date, first vs. repeat purchase, and last activity where available, and it shows how it assigned each group.
- **Drafts the stage-right email**: welcome (set expectations, one clear next step), onboarding (help them get value, not a hard sell), post-first-purchase (reassurance + what's next), re-engagement (a reason to come back, honest, no guilt-trip). Each in a plain, on-brand voice you can edit.
- **Sequences and times them**: suggests the order and spacing per stage (e.g. welcome now, onboarding over the first week) so it's a flow, not a blast.
- **Keeps it honest and compliant**: one primary call to action per email, respects unsubscribe, no fake urgency, and it flags where a message alone won't move the stage (e.g. re-engagement can't fix a product someone stopped needing).
- **Where it stops**: it writes and organizes the copy; it does not send, does not manage an ESP, and can only assign stages from the signals you provide. It closes each run with **the single next email to ship first** — not a score, because drafting copy is a writing task, not a go/no-go decision. If you want a spend/decision call (who's revivable, who to target), pair it with Customer Win-Back or RFM Segmentation.

## How to use

1. Connect HubSpot/Gmail contacts or paste a list with signup dates and purchase history where you have them.
2. Tell it your product and the stages you care about (some businesses skip onboarding; some live on re-engagement).
3. Ask for the stage mapping first, then the email per stage with suggested timing.
4. Edit the voice to yours, drop into Gmail/your CRM, and turn one stage on at a time.
5. Watch opens/replies and unsubscribes per stage; refine the copy before adding more stages.

## Best for

Owners and small marketing teams who email one-size-fits-all and want stage-aware messages without a lifecycle-automation platform. Best when you have signup dates and purchase history; weaker when you have only a flat email list with no context.
