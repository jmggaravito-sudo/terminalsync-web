You've done it hundreds of times. Open a new AI chat. Paste in the context. Upload the relevant files. Explain what the project is about. Wait for the AI to catch up.

Then, next session, do it all over again.

This isn't a minor annoyance — it's a fundamental design problem with how most AI tools work today.

## Why AI chats forget everything

Most AI tools are built around the session metaphor: you start a conversation, the AI helps you, you close the tab, and the AI forgets everything. This made sense when AI was a novelty. It makes no sense as a daily work tool.

Your company doesn't work in sessions. Your clients, projects, and processes are persistent. They build on each other. The problem isn't that AI is forgetting — it's that your AI workspace wasn't designed to remember.

## What "project memory" actually means

A proper AI workspace doesn't just remember your chat history. It maintains:

- **The files** that define the project — docs, specs, reports, data
- **The context** of what the project is and where it stands
- **The decisions** that have already been made
- **The tools** connected to that project

When you open a workspace for "Client X", the AI already knows everything relevant to Client X. You don't explain. You just work.

## The real cost of context repetition

Think about a consulting firm running 20 active client engagements. If each engagement requires 10 minutes of context-setting per AI session, and teams use AI 3 times per day, that's **10 hours of wasted time per day** just re-explaining things the AI should already know.

That's not a small problem. That's a structural tax on every AI-assisted workflow.
